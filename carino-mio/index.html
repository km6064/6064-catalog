Passive ribbon microphone

The warmth of a classic ribbon.
Tuned for clarity and balance.

Designed with simplicity, modularity, and long-term repairability in mind - without compromise on sound quality.

Construction

Monolithic Precision

A single CNC-machined aluminium assembly houses the ribbon motor, transformer, and output XLR. A deliberately integrated design.

The ridges and grooves on the nylon body provide functional alignment of the internal assembly, and easy visual reference for stereo microphone positioning.

Materials

Considered Choices

Every material selected for longevity, repairability, and acoustic performance.

PA12 Nylon

Body and hood SLS printed from PA12 Nylon with shot-peen polish. Chosen for durability, toughness, and precise tolerance matching to the CAD model.

Nextel Suede

Professionally applied Nextel® Suede coating. A tactile, premium matte finish with a refined, low sheen texture.

Lundahl Transformer

Paired with N42 Neodymium magnets and a 1.8 micron aluminium ribbon, tuned for efficiency, clarity, and that unmistakable ribbon warmth.

Aluminium Assembly

CNC-machined motor mount provides rigidity and longevity. A single, serviceable unit integrates the transformer, XLR, magnets, and wiring, with geometry designed for acoustic efficiency.

Mesh Screen

High-performance Saati fabric bonded to a perforated aluminium mesh screen protects against dust and plosives while preserving acoustic transparency.

Neutrik XLR

Rear-exiting, professional-grade connector allows freestanding use or simple mounting with standard clamps.

Finishing

The Ribbon

A 38mm × 5mm ribbon of 1.8 micron aluminium foil was chosen and precisely corrugated. The ribbon, motor, magnets, and transformer were developed in parallel, each element tuned to complement the others.

The final ribbon was professionally installed and tuned by Stewart Tavener (Xaudia/Extinct Audio), a world renowned ribbon microphone expert.
